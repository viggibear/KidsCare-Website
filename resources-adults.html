A compilation of Parenting Resources

Parenting is hard but why do it alone? Everyone has experienced it, almost everyone has to do it - so why not take the best parts of shared wisdom to apply it to your parenting style. Here, we have selected the best of the best resources, the ones that go into detail about different styles of parenting and how to choose one that benefits you and your child's development.

A child is not a lamp to be filled, but a vessel to be lit.

Hebrew Proverb

Links

A Bible on Positive Parenting

This is a comprehensive article written by Heather Lonczak, a clinical psychologist who talks about Positive Parenting at length and provides many resources that talk about parenting children through various scenarios, and also shows the various benefits of using Positive Parenting.

 Positive Discipline

This is a set of principles provided by Jane Nelsen on Positive Discipline and how to manage outbursts and negative behaviour in children. She also has various books that dive deeper into how to manage specific issues in children and teenagers respectively, paying close attention to the various needs of the different age groups.

 Singapore Children's Society

The Children's Society has done much research into various topics about child development and parenting. Check the link to find out more about topics that affect children in their development and how to navigate around these issues.

 The Global Initiative to End All Corporal Punishment of Children

This initiative group aims to end the corporal punishment of all children around the world through legislative and social means by extolling the negatives that come about by using physical discipline while providing alternatives. They also brief and review work on Children's Rights by international bodies, providing invaluable legal insight into this topic.
